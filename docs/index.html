Abstract

We predict lift and drag (Cl, Cd) along angle-of-attack sweeps directly from airfoil contours. The contour is tokenized into 167 non-overlapping triplets of 501 surface stations, embedded in 8-D; a causal four-layer transformer with pairwise outer-product blocks (4,470 parameters) decodes the polar autoregressively with ground-truth AoA conditioning. A residual 34,290-parameter convolutional MLP then refines the polar using the full-resolution geometry. On 1,178 validation airfoils (60% train split, seed 42), the corrected model reaches mean absolute errors of 0.0404 on Cl and 0.0081 on Cd, with L/D error 4.88 vs. 6.01 for the transformer alone—roughly −46% relative improvement on Cl. Compared to NeuralFoil (xxxlarge) on the same split, dataset-trained FoilForm attains roughly 13× lower Cl MAE (0.0404 vs. 0.524) and about 6× lower L/D MAE, at ~0.3 ms vs. ~1.6 ms per airfoil.
